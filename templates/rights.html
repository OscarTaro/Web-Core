Freedom of Association, Assembly, Demonstration and Petition

This right allows individuals to form groups, meet in public, demonstrate peacefully, and submit petitions to authorities without fear of repression.

More details about this right

According to Section 58 of the Zimbabwean Constitution:

"Every person has the right to freedom of assembly and association, and the right not to assemble or associate with others."

This means you can:

  • Form or join any political party, trade union, or organization
  • Gather peacefully with others in public or private spaces
  • Organize or participate in peaceful demonstrations
  • Present petitions to public authorities

Limitations: These rights may be restricted for public safety, public order, public health or morality, but any restrictions must be reasonable and justifiable in a democratic society.

Constitutional reference: Section 58, Zimbabwe Constitution (2013)

Right to Personal Liberty

Protection from arbitrary arrest or detention. Everyone has the right to move freely unless lawfully restricted.

More details about this right

Section 49 of the Constitution states:

"Every person has the right to personal liberty, which includes the right not to be detained without trial."

Key protections include:

  • You cannot be arrested without reasonable suspicion of committing a crime
  • If arrested, you must be brought before a court within 48 hours
  • You have the right to remain silent and not incriminate yourself
  • You have the right to legal representation
  • You cannot be detained longer than the maximum period for the alleged offense

Exception: These rights may be suspended during a state of emergency declared by Parliament.

Constitutional reference: Section 49, Zimbabwe Constitution (2013)

Freedom of Conscience

The freedom to think independently, hold personal beliefs, and practice religion freely.

More details about this right

Section 60 of the Constitution guarantees:

"Every person has the right to freedom of conscience, which includes freedom of thought, opinion, religion or belief."

This right protects:

  • Freedom to change your religion or beliefs
  • Freedom to manifest your religion through worship, practice, teaching
  • Freedom from being compelled to take an oath contrary to your beliefs
  • Parents' rights to provide moral and religious education for their children

Note: Religious practices must not infringe on others' rights or endanger public health/safety.

Constitutional reference: Section 60, Zimbabwe Constitution (2013)

Freedom of Expression, Media Freedom and Access to Information

Guarantees the right to speak freely, access media and information without interference.

More details about this right

Section 61 states:

"Every person has the right to freedom of expression, which includes freedom to seek, receive and communicate ideas and information."

This includes:

  • Freedom of the press and other media
  • Academic freedom in institutions of higher education
  • Freedom to access information held by the state or public institutions
  • Right to communicate in any language of your choice

Limitations: These rights may be limited to protect national security, public order, public health or morals, or the rights of others.

Constitutional reference: Sections 61 & 62, Zimbabwe Constitution (2013)

Right to Life

Every person has the inherent right to life, which must be protected by law.

More details about this right

Section 48 declares:

"Every person has the right to life. No person may be deprived of life intentionally except in execution of a sentence of a court after conviction for an offense."

Important aspects:

  • Death penalty is permitted only for murder committed in aggravating circumstances
  • Cannot be imposed on women, persons under 21 or over 70
  • State must protect lives through reasonable legislative measures
  • Includes right to access healthcare, clean water, and adequate food

Constitutional reference: Section 48, Zimbabwe Constitution (2013)

Right to Human Dignity

Everyone deserves to be treated with respect, worth, and without humiliation.

More details about this right

Section 51 states:

"Every person has inherent dignity in their private and public life, and the right to have that dignity respected and protected."

This right protects against:

  • Degrading treatment or punishment
  • Unfair discrimination
  • Violation of bodily integrity
  • Forced labor or servitude

It also guarantees:

  • Right to reasonable accommodation for persons with disabilities
  • Right to fair labor practices
  • Protection from slavery and human trafficking

Constitutional reference: Section 51, Zimbabwe Constitution (2013)

Right to Privacy

This right protects private life, personal data, and communications.

More details about this right

Section 57 provides:

"Every person has the right to privacy, which includes the right not to have their home, premises or property entered without their permission."

This includes:

  • Privacy of personal communications (mail, phone, email)
  • Protection of personal data and information
  • Freedom from unlawful searches of person or property
  • Right to confidentiality of health information

Exceptions: Law enforcement may conduct searches with a valid warrant or in urgent circumstances.

Constitutional reference: Section 57, Zimbabwe Constitution (2013)

Freedom from Torture or Cruel, Inhuman or Degrading Treatment or Punishment

No one shall be subjected to torture or to cruel, inhuman or degrading punishment or treatment.

More details about this right

Section 53 states:

"No person may be subjected to physical or psychological torture or to cruel, inhuman or degrading treatment or punishment."

This absolute right prohibits:

  • Any form of physical abuse by authorities
  • Psychological torment or coercion
  • Harsh prison conditions that violate human dignity
  • Medical or scientific experimentation without consent

Important: This right has no exceptions and cannot be limited under any circumstances, including states of emergency.

Constitutional reference: Section 53, Zimbabwe Constitution (2013)

Right to Fair Hearing

Everyone has the right to a fair and public hearing by an impartial body.

More details about this right

Section 69 provides:

"Every person accused of an offense has the right to a fair and public trial within a reasonable time before an independent and impartial court."

Key elements include:

  • Presumption of innocence until proven guilty
  • Right to be informed of charges in a language you understand
  • Right to adequate time and facilities to prepare defense
  • Right to legal representation (state-funded if necessary)
  • Right to examine witnesses and present evidence
  • Right to appeal or review of conviction/sentence

Constitutional reference: Section 69, Zimbabwe Constitution (2013)

Right to Administrative Justice

Protection from unfair decisions by authorities; ensures lawful and timely actions.

More details about this right

Section 68 states:

"Every person has a right to administrative conduct that is lawful, prompt, efficient, reasonable, proportionate, impartial and both substantively and procedurally fair."

This means you have the right to:

  • Be given written reasons for administrative decisions affecting you
  • Request review of adverse administrative decisions
  • Access information relevant to administrative decisions about you
  • Expect government officials to act within their legal authority

Application: Covers all government departments, local authorities, and public institutions.

Constitutional reference: Section 68, Zimbabwe Constitution (2013)

Freedom from Arbitrary Eviction

You cannot be removed from your home or land without due legal process.

More details about this right

Section 74 provides:

"No person may be evicted from their home, or have their home demolished, without an order of court made after considering all relevant circumstances."

Protections include:

  • Requirement for court order before eviction
  • Right to be heard before eviction order is granted
  • Prohibition on destruction of crops or property during eviction
  • Right to alternative accommodation if evicted from public land

Exception: Emergency situations may allow temporary relocation for public safety, but with right to return when safe.

Constitutional reference: Section 74, Zimbabwe Constitution (2013)
