Connect Aid Privacy Policy

Last Updated: June 2025

1. Our Commitment to Your Privacy

Connect Aid provides this Privacy Policy to explain how we collect, use, protect, and disclose information when you use our legal support services in Zimbabwe. We are committed to maintaining the confidentiality of your personal information and case details in accordance with:

  • The Constitution of Zimbabwe (Section 57 - Right to Privacy)
  • Data Protection Act [Chapter 11:12]
  • Legal Practitioners Act
All legal case information is protected by attorney-client privilege where applicable.

2. Information We Collect

Personal Identification Information

When accessing our legal support services, we may collect:

  • Contact details (phone, encrypted email)
  • Demographic information needed for legal aid qualification
  • Case details you choose to disclose
  • Government-issued ID (only when required for court proceedings)

Non-Personal Information

For service improvement, we may collect:

  • Browser type and device information
  • Anonymous usage statistics
  • De-identified case categories for reporting

3. How We Protect Your Information

We implement security measures appropriate for legal service providers:

  • Encryption: All case communications use end-to-end encryption
  • Access Controls: Strict staff access limitations to case files
  • Secure Storage: On-premise servers with biometric access
  • Redaction: Automatic removal of identifying details in public reports
In high-risk cases, we offer anonymous reporting options through our secure portal.

4. How We Use Your Information

Your information helps us provide effective legal support:

  • To connect you with appropriate legal resources
  • To document human rights violations (with consent)
  • To qualify you for pro bono legal services
  • To generate aggregate reports for advocacy (never identifying individuals)
  • To improve our services

Zimbabwean law prohibits disclosure of client information without consent except where required by court order (Legal Practitioners Act Section 56).

5. Your Privacy Rights

Under Zimbabwean law, you have the right to:

  • Access your personal information we hold
  • Request correction of inaccurate information
  • Withdraw consent for data processing (where applicable)
  • Request deletion of non-essential personal data
  • File a complaint with the Zimbabwe Human Rights Commission
Note: Certain legal case records must be retained for statutory periods even upon request for deletion.

6. Contact Our Privacy Officer

For privacy concerns or to exercise your rights:

Data Protection Officer
Connect Aid Zimbabwe
Email: privacy@connectaid.org
Secure Portal: Privacy Request Form

We respond to all legitimate requests within 14 working days as required by law.
